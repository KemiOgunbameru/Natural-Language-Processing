Patience is the ability to wait calmly for results, endure difficult situations, and tolerate delays without becoming frustrated or anxious. It is considered a virtue in many cultures and religions around the world. Patience allows individuals to think clearly and make better decisions rather than acting impulsively.

In psychology, patience is closely related to emotional regulation and self-control. People who practice patience tend to have lower stress levels, better mental health, and stronger relationships. Research has shown that patient individuals are more likely to achieve long-term goals because they are willing to persist through challenges.

Patience can be developed through mindfulness meditation, deep breathing exercises, and consciously choosing to slow down in stressful situations. It is especially important in personal relationships, where understanding and tolerating the shortcomings of others leads to deeper and more meaningful connections.
